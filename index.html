The Gayle and Tom Benson Ochsner Children's Hospital wouldn't be possible without the generosity of our donors, especially Owner of the New Orleans Saints and Governor of the New Orleans Pelicans Mrs. Gayle Benson. Her belief in this mission and in the future of Louisiana's children is what made this hospital a reality.

To every person, foundation and organization that has invested in this vision, we thank you. Your support is building a healthier, brighter future for generations to come!

Debra H. and Robert J. Patrick Neuroscience Institute, a state-of-the-art facility that will transform how we care for patients with brain and spine conditions. Now at its halfway point, this purpose-built space is redefining care by combining advanced diagnostics,

research, and holistic therapies under one roof. With calming artwork, therapeutic greenspaces, and patient-friendly design elements, the Institute will offer a welcoming, supportive environment that reflects our commitment to treating the whole person. Together, we are shaping a future where complex neurological challenges are met with compassion, innovation, and world-class care.

Champions of Change

Philanthropy at Work

Powering Transplant Innovation

The Lipsey Family Foundation's generous gift to Ochsner's Transplant Institute is revolutionizing organ transplantation. With funding from the foundation, Ochsner is investing in state-of-the-art organ preservation technologies that enhance transplant viability, save more lives and extend the reach of care.

Fueling the Future of Nursing

Senior VP and Chief Nursing Officer Tiffany Murdock is giving back to her profession. Her $5,000 gift to the Nursing Empowerment Fund strengthens innovation at the bedside, supports wellness initiatives and enhances professional development for nurses.

Opening Doors for Neurodiverse Youth

Thanks to a $100,000 grant from Valero's Benefit for Children program, Ochsner's Boh Center for Child Development can support 20 individuals in its Vocational Social Skills Program. Participants gain vital workplace skills, preparing them for integrated employment.
